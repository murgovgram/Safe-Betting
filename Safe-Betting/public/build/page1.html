How Do Acca Insurance Offers Work?

Bookies operate differently when it comes to accumulator insurance offers. Firstly, punters must take into account the number of selections required in order to qualify. 4 or 5-fold accumulator bets are the most common, however this can differ slightly with some operators. Meanwhile, minimum odds requirements are also likely to be in place. Each selection must meet a certain price, while the total odds must also be equal to or above a set amount. This is likely to be around 1/3 for each leg and 4/1 for the total acca price. Finally, punters will receive their stake back, up to a set amount, either as a full refund or free bet in most cases. This varies significantly, however it is normally anywhere between £10 and £50. The following are some examples of how such offers work:

Example 1:

BYou place an accumulator bet worth £10 on both teams to score in four English Premier League football matches. The first three matches go to plan, with all six teams finding the back of the net. However, in the final game, the game ends 1-0, meaning that the acca ultimately loses. However, as a result of meeting the acca insurance qualifying conditions, you will receive your stake back in the form of a free bet. This £10 free bet will then be available on a sports market of your choice for the next 7 days.
